CebuTech Danao Transforms Lives Through Blood Donation Drives

CTU - Danao Campus 3 min read • Sep 27, 2024
101 4

The National Service Training Program (NSTP) office of Cebu Technological University (CTU) Danao Campus, in partnership with Vicente Sotto Memorial Medical Center (VSMMC), is holding a three-day Blood Donation Drive. This event, running from September 26 to 28, 2024, takes place at the CME Building on campus and is designed to engage the entire CTU community—students, faculty, and staff alike. It aims not only to boost the blood supply in the region but also to raise awareness about the importance of blood donation and foster a spirit of compassion and community involvement.

The drive highlights the critical role blood donations play in meeting healthcare needs. Blood is essential for surgeries, medical treatments, and emergency interventions, and every donation can mean the difference between life and death for patients in need. By donating blood, participants perform an act of kindness that directly contributes to saving lives, improving health outcomes, and supporting the medical community.

As of the second day of the Blood Donation Drive, 137 successful donors have contributed to the effort, with more expected to participate on the final day. These donors represent a cross-section of the campus community, unified by the shared goal of making a tangible difference. The turnout reflects the deep sense of social responsibility embedded in the university's culture.

Through initiatives like this, CTU Danao Campus is not just fulfilling its educational mission but also nurturing a strong sense of solidarity among its members. The event underscores how collective action, such as giving blood, can strengthen the bonds within the community and remind everyone that a single donation has the power to save multiple lives.

The Blood Donation Drive, therefore, serves as both a practical healthcare intervention and a powerful symbol of the university's commitment to fostering a compassionate and engaged society. As the final day of the event approaches, organizers remain optimistic that even more members of the CTU Danao community will step forward to donate, ensuring that the blood collected can continue to meet the needs of hospitals and patients long after the event concludes.
