6th International Conference on Automation, Mechatronics, and Robotics (ICAMEROB) 2024 Kicks Off in CebuTech Danao

The 6th International Conference on Automation, Mechatronics, and Robotics (ICAMEROB) 2024 commenced today at Cebu Technological University Danao Campus, attracting a diverse group of scholars, industry professionals, and enthusiasts from across the globe. The conference runs from September 26 to 28 and features a rich program of discussions aimed at advancing the fields of automation and robotics.

The event began with a processional of esteemed guests and plenary speakers, showcasing representatives from Finland, Malaysia, Singapore, and the Philippines. Dr. Joseph Pepito, President of CTU, warmly welcomed participants, emphasizing the university's commitment to fostering innovation and collaboration in technology-driven fields. "We are thrilled to host ICAMEROB 2024 and bring together some of the brightest minds in automation and robotics," Dr. Pepito stated. "This conference is a testament to our dedication to advancing technology that can improve lives and transform industries."

The keynote address was delivered by Mr. Ramon Gorre Jr., head of Danao City's Planning and Development Office (CPDO) standing in for Hon. Mayor Thomas Durano of the City of Danao. Mr. Gorre highlighted the city's commitment to technological advancement and its role in promoting a sustainable and innovative community through its partner stakeholders.

The plenary sessions featured distinguished speakers who offered insights into the latest trends and technologies in automation and robotics. The first plenary speech was presented by Prof. Vesa Salminen from HAMK Häme University of Applied Sciences in Finland. His talk focused on the integration of mechatronics in modern manufacturing processes, highlighting how interdisciplinary approaches can enhance efficiency and productivity. Following Salminen, Dr. Ishkandar Baharin, President of the Malaysia Robotics and Automation Society, discussed the booming field of robotics in Southeast Asia. He emphasized the need for robust educational frameworks to prepare future engineers and technologists who can drive the region's industrial growth. The final plenary speaker, Mr. Oliver Tian from the Asia Pacific Assistive Robotics Association (APARA) in Singapore, addressed the crucial role of assistive robotics in improving quality of life for individuals with disabilities. His presentation underscored the ethical implications and societal benefits of robotics technologies that focus on human assistance.

The discussions surrounding automation, mechatronics, and robotics are increasingly relevant in today's rapidly evolving technological landscape. As industries move towards greater efficiency, there is a growing demand for innovative solutions that can enhance productivity while addressing societal needs. This conference serves as a platform for knowledge exchange and collaboration among researchers and practitioners, ultimately fostering advancements that will shape the future of automation and robotics.

ICAMEROB 2024 promises to be a dynamic and informative event, to hasten new partnerships and pioneering research that will propel the fields of automation and robotics to new heights. Participants will engage in workshops, panel discussions, research presentations and networking opportunities over the next few days, further contributing to the vibrant dialogue in this critical area of technology.
