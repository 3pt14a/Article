200 8

Ribbon cutting ceremony marks official opening of CebuTech Danao RFID system

In a significant stride towards modernizing campus operations, Cebu Technological University- Danao Campus holds a ribbon-cutting ceremony on September 18, 2024, to officially launch its new Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) system. The event, spearheaded by campus director Dr. Rose Mary L. Almacen, brought together various stakeholders, including Danao City Mayor Thomas Durano and Globe Telecom Head Senior Manager, Miss Ginalyn Villaceran, alongside alumni, parents, faculty, staff, and students.

The ceremony commenced with Dr. Almacen addressing the attendees, emphasizing the transformative potential of the RFID technology. “Today, we are taking a vital step towards enhancing our campus operations,” said Dr. Almacen. “This RFID system will not only streamline our administrative processes but also improve the overall safety and experience for our students and staff.”

Hon. Thomas “Mix” Durano (Danao City Mayor) commended the partnership between the Local Government Unit and the university recognizing the initiative which exemplifies the commitment of both the university and the LGU to foster a technologically advanced learning environment. Hon. Durano states, “We've been moving into our objective having a centralized system in providing service to our people and this success of your school catapults our plan. The local government is willing to support your endeavors to provide the best learning environment to our students, our future leaders.”

Miss Villaceran echoed these sentiments, noting that Globe Telecom is committed to empower educational institutions through technology. “Our partnership with CebuTech Danao represents a shared vision for a smarter, more connected educational landscape,” she stated. “We are excited to see how this RFID system will enhance learning and administrative processes.”

The introduction of the RFID system is anticipated to significantly reduce queuing times, improve accuracy in attendance tracking, and enhance security through real-time monitoring of campus resources which fosters a more engaging academic atmosphere.
